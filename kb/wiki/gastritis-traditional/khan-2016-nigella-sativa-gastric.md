## TL;DR

Reviews the use of Nigella sativa (black seed / Habbatus sauda) for gastric ailments across Unani, Islamic, and folk medical traditions. Covers the pharmacological evidence for its gastroprotective, anti-inflammatory, and anti-secretory effects, with thymoquinone identified as the primary active compound.

## Key concepts

- **Nigella sativa (black seed)** — One of the most revered medicinal plants in Unani/Islamic medicine; traditionally used for a wide range of ailments including gastric disorders.
- **Thymoquinone** — The principal bioactive compound in Nigella sativa oil, with anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and gastroprotective properties.
