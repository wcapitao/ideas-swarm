## TL;DR

Demonstrates the gastroprotective mechanism of thymoquinone, the active compound from Nigella sativa, in animal models of gastric ulceration. Shows that thymoquinone protects gastric mucosa through enhanced prostaglandin synthesis, reduced oxidative stress, and anti-inflammatory effects, providing mechanistic validation for the traditional Unani use of black seed for stomach ailments.

## Key concepts

- **Thymoquinone gastroprotection** — Mechanistic evidence that thymoquinone from Nigella sativa protects gastric mucosa via prostaglandin and antioxidant pathways.
- **Prostaglandin-mediated mucosal defense** — Thymoquinone enhances endogenous prostaglandin E2 production, strengthening the mucosal barrier.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator

Provides the molecular mechanism linking a traditional remedy to a well-characterized gastroprotective pathway (prostaglandins), enabling direct comparison with NSAID-induced gastric damage models. Builds comprehensive gastritis understanding across paradigms.
