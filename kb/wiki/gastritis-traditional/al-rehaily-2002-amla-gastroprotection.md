## TL;DR

Demonstrates the gastroprotective effects of Amla (Emblica officinalis / Phyllanthus emblica) in animal models of gastric ulceration. Shows that this key Ayurvedic Rasayana fruit reduces gastric mucosal damage through antioxidant and cytoprotective mechanisms, validating its traditional use in digestive disorders.

## Key concepts

- **Amla (Emblica officinalis)** — Indian gooseberry; one of the most important Ayurvedic Rasayana (rejuvenating) herbs, rich in vitamin C and tannins.
- **Gastroprotection via antioxidant mechanism** — Amla's high polyphenol content scavenges free radicals and protects gastric mucosa from oxidative damage.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator

Amla appears in multiple Ayurvedic formulas (notably Triphala), making it a hub compound for understanding Ayurvedic polypharmacy logic. Builds comprehensive gastritis understanding across paradigms.
