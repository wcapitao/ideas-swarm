## TL;DR

Analyzes the historical record of acupoint selection for gastritis across classical TCM texts, identifying the most frequently prescribed points (notably Zusanli ST36 and Zhongwan CV12) and their evolution over centuries. Provides a data-mining perspective on traditional acupuncture practice for gastrointestinal disorders.

## Key concepts

- **Zusanli (ST36)** — The most commonly prescribed acupoint for gastric disorders across historical texts; located on the stomach meridian.
- **Zhongwan (CV12)** — Front-mu point of the stomach, historically paired with ST36 for gastritis treatment.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator

Demonstrates that even within traditional systems, empirical data mining can reveal convergent therapeutic choices, analogous to combinatorial optimization. Builds comprehensive gastritis understanding across paradigms.
