## TL;DR

Demonstrates the anti-*H. pylori* activity of mastic gum (Chios mastic, from Pistacia lentiscus) in an animal model, validating the ancient Greco-Roman use of this resin for gastric complaints. Identifies specific triterpenic acids as the active antibacterial components responsible for H. pylori eradication.

## Key concepts

- **Mastic gum (Chios mastic)** — Resin from Pistacia lentiscus var. chia, used since antiquity in the eastern Mediterranean for stomach ailments; referenced by Dioscorides.
- **Triterpenic acids** — Active anti-H. pylori compounds in mastic gum, including isomasticadienonic acid.
