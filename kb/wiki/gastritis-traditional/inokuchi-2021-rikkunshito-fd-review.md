## TL;DR

Comprehensive review of Rikkunshito (Liu Jun Zi Tang), the most studied Kampo formula for functional dyspepsia. Covers its pharmacological mechanisms including ghrelin signaling enhancement, gastric accommodation improvement, and anti-inflammatory effects. Synthesizes clinical trial evidence supporting its use in guideline-recommended treatment of FD.

## Key concepts

- **Rikkunshito** — Six-herb Kampo formula (the Japanese version of TCM's Liu Jun Zi Tang) for functional dyspepsia and gastroparesis.
- **Ghrelin signaling** — Rikkunshito enhances endogenous ghrelin release, improving appetite and gastric motility.
- **Gastric accommodation** — Relaxation of the proximal stomach to accept food; impaired in functional dyspepsia.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator

Rikkunshito is the gold standard for Kampo gastritis research, with mechanism-level understanding comparable to pharmaceutical drugs. Provides a benchmark for evaluating other traditional formulas. Builds comprehensive gastritis understanding across paradigms.
