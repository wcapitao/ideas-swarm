## TL;DR

Reviews the clinical efficacy and proposed mechanisms of acupuncture for chronic gastritis, including neuromodulation of the vagus nerve, anti-inflammatory cytokine regulation, and gastric motility enhancement. Synthesizes evidence from multiple trial designs to evaluate acupuncture as a standalone or adjunctive gastritis therapy.

## Key concepts

- **Acupuncture for gastritis** — Needle stimulation at specific body points to modulate gastric function and inflammation.
- **Vagal neuromodulation** — Acupuncture-induced activation of vagal afferents affecting gastric acid secretion and motility.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator

Introduces a non-pharmacological TCM modality with a distinct mechanism of action (neuromodulation vs. biochemical), expanding the combinatorial space beyond herbal formulas. Builds comprehensive gastritis understanding across paradigms.
