## TL;DR

Early pharmacological study demonstrating the gastroprotective effects of ginger (Zingiber officinale) in animal models of gastric ulceration. Provides foundational in-vivo evidence for one of the most universally used medicinal plants in traditional gastric medicine, used across folk, Ayurvedic, TCM, and Unani traditions.

## Key concepts

- **Ginger gastroprotection** — Anti-ulcer activity of ginger extract in experimentally induced gastric lesions.
- **Universal medicinal plant** — Ginger is arguably the most cross-culturally used gastroprotective plant, appearing in virtually every traditional medicine system.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator

As one of the earliest modern pharmacological validations of ginger's gastroprotective activity, this study anchors the evidence timeline for a plant that appears in every traditional paradigm in the KB. Builds comprehensive gastritis understanding across paradigms.
