## TL;DR

Identifies anti-ulcer compounds from Voacanga africana, a plant used in Cameroonian traditional medicine for gastric complaints. Demonstrates cytoprotective activity of specific alkaloids in animal ulcer models, providing pharmacological validation for a Central African folk remedy.

## Key concepts

- **Voacanga africana** — West/Central African tree whose bark and seeds are used in traditional medicine; contains indole alkaloids with gastroprotective activity.
- **Anti-ulcer alkaloids** — Specific alkaloid compounds from V. africana that protect gastric mucosa in experimental ulcer models.
