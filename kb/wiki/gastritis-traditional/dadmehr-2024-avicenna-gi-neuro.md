## TL;DR

Examines Avicenna's writings on the connection between gastrointestinal and neurological function, finding that the Canon of Medicine anticipated modern gut-brain axis concepts. Reviews Avicenna's classification of gastric symptoms with neurological components and his therapeutic strategies addressing both systems simultaneously.

## Key concepts

- **Avicenna's gut-brain connection** — Ibn Sina described bidirectional influence between stomach and brain via temperamental and humoral pathways.
- **Historical neurogastroenterology** — Pre-modern recognition that gastric and neurological disorders share pathways and respond to combined treatment.
