## TL;DR

Demonstrates the gastroprotective effects of turmeric and ginger essential oils in animal models of gastric ulceration. Shows that these Ayurvedic culinary-medicinal spices protect gastric mucosa through anti-inflammatory and antioxidant mechanisms, supporting the traditional dietary-therapeutic approach of Ayurveda.

## Key concepts

- **Turmeric (Curcuma longa)** — Key Ayurvedic spice-medicine with anti-inflammatory gastroprotective essential oil components beyond curcumin.
- **Ginger (Zingiber officinale)** — Used across virtually all traditional medicine systems for nausea and gastric complaints; essential oil fraction shows gastroprotection.
- **Ayurvedic dietary therapeutics** — The Ayurvedic principle that food and medicine exist on a continuum, with spices serving dual roles.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator

Turmeric and ginger bridge the food-medicine divide across multiple traditions, offering a model for how dietary interventions can be systematically compared with pharmacological ones. Builds comprehensive gastritis understanding across paradigms.
