## TL;DR

Applies network pharmacology analysis to Agastache rugosa (Korean mint), a key herb in Korean traditional medicine used for gastric disorders. Identifies multi-target mechanisms of action through computational prediction validated by in-vitro experiments, demonstrating anti-inflammatory and gastroprotective pathway engagement.

## Key concepts

- **Agastache rugosa** — Korean medicinal herb (Gwak-hyang) used for gastric disorders, nausea, and digestive weakness in Korean traditional medicine.
- **Network pharmacology** — Computational approach modeling multi-component, multi-target interactions of herbal medicines; a modern tool for understanding traditional polypharmacy.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator

Network pharmacology is itself a combinatorial analysis method, making this study a methodological template for how computational approaches can decode traditional medicine logic. Builds comprehensive gastritis understanding across paradigms.
