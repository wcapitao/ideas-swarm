## TL;DR

Surveys African natural products used traditionally for gastric ulcers, cataloging plants across multiple African healing traditions with documented gastroprotective activity. Provides an ethnobotanical overview of the continent's diverse anti-ulcer pharmacopeia, identifying both well-studied and under-researched species.

## Key concepts

- **African anti-ulcer ethnobotany** — Systematic documentation of plants used across African traditional healing systems for gastric ulcers.
- **Gastroprotective plant diversity** — Africa's botanical diversity yields a large number of candidate gastroprotective species, many unstudied pharmacologically.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator

Opens a largely untapped pharmacopeia for cross-paradigm comparison, where most entries lack the mechanistic characterization available for TCM or Ayurvedic herbs. Builds comprehensive gastritis understanding across paradigms.
