## TL;DR
Studies whether LLMs can supply useful cross-domain analogies to a human problem-solver. Median helpfulness 4/5; reformulates the problem in ~80% of cases. ~25% of analogies were rated 'potentially harmful' (mostly upsetting content rather than biased).

## Key thesis
Cross-domain analogy is the cognitive mechanism behind combinatorial creativity. LLMs can productively augment human analogy-making — and the value isn't the polished analogy itself but the *perspective shift* the analogy induces. The 80% reformulation rate is the headline finding.

## Key concepts
- **cross-domain analogy** — the Gentner/Hofstadter operation, surfaced by an LLM.
- **perspective shift** — the value isn't the analogy but its effect on framing.
- **harm rating of creative outputs** — quantifies the safety risk of unconstrained creativity.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator
Earliest evidence that LLMs can productively augment cross-domain analogical creativity for humans. Two implications for ai-ideator: (1) generating analogies is a high-leverage move even when the analogy itself is imperfect — the perspective shift carries the value, (2) safety review should expect ~25% of creative outputs to be problematic in some way.

## Memorable findings
