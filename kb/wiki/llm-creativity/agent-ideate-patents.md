## TL;DR
Mines patents to generate downstream product ideas using open-source LLMs vs. agentic architectures across CS, NLP, and Material Chemistry. **Agentic workflows consistently beat standalone LLMs** on idea quality, relevance, and novelty.

## Key thesis
Patents encode under-exploited combinations the market has not realized — they are a high-signal substrate for product ideation. Agentic decomposition (analyzer → extractor → ideator → critic) outperforms single-shot LLM use, even with weaker base models.

## Key concepts
- **patent-grounded ideation** — patents as concept substrate.
- **agentic decomposition** — workflow > single prompt.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator
One of the few papers targeting **business/product ideation** rather than scientific ideation. Patents as an idea substrate are a strong signal: they codify under-exploited concept combinations (Sternberg's "buy low, sell high" applies). ai-ideator should consider patent corpora as a Phase-1 augmentation.

## Memorable findings
> "Agentic architectures beat standalone LLMs for turning patents into product ideas — the value-add is the workflow, not just the model."
