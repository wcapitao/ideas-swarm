## TL;DR
Pre-registered N=1,100 experiments across three conditions (no-LLM, standard LLM, coach-LLM) on Alternate Uses Test (divergent) and Remote Associates Test (convergent). LLM use boosts performance during the assisted task but participants then perform **worse than controls** when working unassisted.

## Key thesis
LLM-assisted ideation is not a free lunch for the user. Short-term performance goes up; longitudinal creative skill goes down. Coach-mode prompting (where the LLM asks rather than answers) partially mitigates the atrophy effect.

## Key concepts
- **creativity-skill atrophy** — measurable post-assistance decline.
- **coach mode vs. answer mode** — prompting style matters for long-term user skill.
- **AUT / RAT** — standard psychometric creativity instruments.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator
Critical countervailing evidence: an ideation system that boosts in-session output may degrade users' creative skills longitudinally. UX/product implications: ai-ideator should default to coach-mode prompting (asking probing questions rather than producing finished ideas) when the human is the primary creative agent. Different mode for "make me a slate of ideas" vs "help me think about X."

## Memorable findings
> "LLM-assisted users beat controls during assistance, then lose to controls afterward — there is a measurable creativity-skill atrophy effect."
