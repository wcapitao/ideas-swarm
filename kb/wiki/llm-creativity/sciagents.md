## TL;DR
Combines a large ontological knowledge graph (materials science) with multiple LLM agents that propose, critique, and refine hypotheses while pulling external data. Targets bio-inspired materials discovery and surfaces interdisciplinary connections previously considered unrelated.

## Key thesis
A large ontological KG is not decorative — it is what produces non-obvious cross-disciplinary combinations. Without the graph, multi-agent systems re-circulate the same ideas; with it, they reach connections that span sub-fields.

## Key concepts
- **ontological KG** — typed entities and relations across a scientific domain.
- **specialized agents swarm** — proposer, critic, retriever, synthesizer roles.
- **in-situ retrieval** — agents pull current literature mid-reasoning.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator
Strongest existing example of KG + multi-agent ideation in a real scientific domain. The lesson: a structured KG over the concept space is force-multiplying for combinatorial creativity, beyond what raw vector retrieval delivers. ai-ideator's Phase 1 (concept ontology) should produce a typed graph, not just an embedding store.

## Memorable findings
> "Hidden cross-disciplinary connections in materials science emerged only when the multi-agent swarm was grounded in a large ontological KG, not from LLM weights alone."
