## TL;DR
Foundational work that defines the three-fold taxonomy of creativity — **combinational, exploratory, and transformational** — and argues all three can in principle be modeled computationally. Establishes the modern computational vocabulary that connects the philosophical lineage (Hume → Koestler) to AI systems.

## Key thesis
Creativity is "the ability to come up with ideas or artefacts that are new, surprising, and valuable." It comes in three structurally distinct forms:
1. **Combinational** — unfamiliar combinations of familiar ideas. The Hume/Koestler engine.
2. **Exploratory** — searching within an existing conceptual space (genre, paradigm, style).
3. **Transformational** — changing the rules of the conceptual space itself.

She also distinguishes **P-creativity** (psychologically novel — new to the agent) from **H-creativity** (historically novel — new to all of human history).

## Key concepts
- **combinational creativity** — produces unfamiliar combinations of familiar ideas; this is the operation an LLM concept-combiner performs.
- **exploratory creativity** — search inside a conceptual space whose rules are fixed.
- **transformational creativity** — alters the rules of the space; rare and deep.
- **conceptual space** — a structured, rule-governed region of possible thoughts/artefacts.
- **P-creativity vs H-creativity** — psychological vs historical novelty.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator
Boden's vocabulary lets the project state explicitly which kind of creativity it targets. ai-ideator is primarily a **combinational** system, with secondary support for **exploratory** moves (variations on a blend) and rare **transformational** ones (constraint-dropping). Without Boden's terminology, the design conflates these — and that conflation is the most common failure mode of "creative" LLM tools.
