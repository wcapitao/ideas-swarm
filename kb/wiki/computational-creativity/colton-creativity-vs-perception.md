## TL;DR
Judging a creative system by output alone is wrong; observers must perceive **skill, appreciation, and imagination** in the producing process. Introduces the **creative tripod** — three legs a system must visibly demonstrate to be perceived as creative.

## Key thesis
A program that produces beautiful poems by accident is not creative; a program that produces mediocre poems with visible care, taste, and exploration may be. Creativity attribution is partly about the artifact and partly about the visibly demonstrated *process*. All three legs of the tripod are required: skill (technique), appreciation (taste/discrimination), imagination (exploration of alternatives).

## Key concepts
- **creative tripod** — skill, appreciation, imagination.
- **framing information** — narrative the system supplies about what it did and why.
- **process matters** — the perception of creativity depends on visible reasoning, not just output.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator
Crucial counterpoint to pure-output evaluation. ai-ideator must make its reasoning *visible*: show that it considered alternatives (imagination), filtered against criteria (appreciation), constructed the idea with technique (skill). This is the architectural argument for the Phase-3 generator-critic split *also* surfacing the rejected candidates and the reasons for rejection — those are the "imagination" and "appreciation" legs of the tripod.

## Memorable passages
> "The way [a system] produces artefacts also needs to be taken into account… it is not appropriate to base the assessment of a system on its output alone."
