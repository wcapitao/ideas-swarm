## TL;DR
Creative individuals "buy low and sell high" in the world of ideas: they pursue ideas that are unknown or out of favor but have growth potential, persist through resistance, then move on once the idea becomes mainstream. Six confluent resources are required: intelligence, knowledge, thinking style, personality, motivation, environment.

## Key thesis
Creativity is an *investment* problem. The most valuable ideas are those currently undervalued by consensus but with high underlying merit. The creator's job is identification (spotting an undervalued idea), commitment (working on it through resistance), and exit (moving on once others catch on).

## Key concepts
- **buy low, sell high** — the core economic metaphor.
- **confluence model** — six resources must align for creative production.
- **defying the crowd** — the willingness to back ideas the consensus rejects.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator
The economic metaphor is directly useful for a business-idea generator: favor combinations that the market has not yet recognized. This is a built-in heuristic for filtering LLM outputs:
- High-value blend = high underlying merit + low current consensus belief.
- Detect "current consensus belief" via retrieval frequency, search-result density, or LLM-judge "have you seen this before?" prompts.

This is the most operationally useful creativity theory for *business* ideation specifically.
