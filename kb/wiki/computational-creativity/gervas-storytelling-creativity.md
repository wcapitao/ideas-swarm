## TL;DR
Surveys computational story-generation systems and evaluates them against the criteria of computational creativity. Argues that narrative generation is a productive testbed for combinational/exploratory creativity because stories are highly structured combinations of characters, events, and themes.

## Key thesis
Story generation is a perfect target domain for combinatorial creativity research: stories have explicit structural slots (character, action, setting, complication, resolution), domain-specific notions of quality (coherence, suspense, theme), and rich existing literature to evaluate against. Pre-LLM systems — TALE-SPIN, MEXICA, etc. — already showed combinatorial generation works.

## Key concepts
- **narrative generation** — composition of stories from structured elements.
- **plot structure** — the high-level frame within which combination occurs.
- **TALE-SPIN, MEXICA** — canonical pre-LLM story generators.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator
Structural parallel to business ideas: both are recombinations of structured elements (character/event/setting vs. customer/value/channel) where coherence and surprise both matter. Gervás shows how to evaluate a generative system against Boden/Ritchie/Wiggins criteria with a concrete domain — a useful template for ai-ideator's evaluation harness.
