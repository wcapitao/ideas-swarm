## TL;DR
Traces the consensus definition of creativity to its origins (Stein 1953; Barron 1955) and crystallizes it: creativity requires both **originality (novelty) AND effectiveness (usefulness, fit, appropriateness)**. Originality alone is bizarreness, not creativity.

## Key thesis
The field has converged — across psychology, design, AI, education — on a two-criterion definition. Either criterion alone is insufficient: novel-but-useless = noise; useful-but-not-novel = competence. Creativity is the conjunction. This is the shortest, most-cited definitional anchor in the field.

## Key concepts
- **standard definition** — novelty AND effectiveness.
- **originality** — distance from existing artefacts/ideas.
- **effectiveness** — usefulness, fit, appropriateness for a purpose.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator
The dual criterion is **the** evaluation function. Drop novelty → you get platitudes. Drop effectiveness → you get gibberish. ai-ideator's evaluator must score both axes independently and combine them — never collapse to one. Cite this paper as the operational definition of what the system maximizes.
