## TL;DR
Demystifies linguistic creativity by showing it is built from concrete computational mechanisms — analogy, metaphor, blending, neologism, cliché reuse — most of which are *recombinative* rather than ex nihilo. Creativity is craft, not magic.

## Key thesis
What we call "creative" language is largely the principled reuse and recombination of existing material under context-sensitive constraints. Veale builds and demonstrates concrete computational systems (Metaphor Magnet, others) that exploit web-scale n-gram resources, simile harvesting, and slot-filling to produce outputs that pass for human creativity.

## Key concepts
- **creative reuse** — the cliché is the raw material, not the enemy.
- **slot-filling templates** — generative recipes for novel-but-coherent linguistic creativity.
- **Metaphor Magnet** — Veale's web-grounded metaphor generator.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator
Veale's central thesis — creativity is mostly principled recombination — is precisely the philosophical license for an LLM concept-combiner. Concrete recipes (web n-grams as a stereotype source, simile harvesting, slot-filling) translate cleanly into LLM prompting strategies for generating novel concept blends.

## Memorable passages
> "Creativity is less about magic than it is about mechanism."
