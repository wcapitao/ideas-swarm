## TL;DR
The **field-defining manifesto** for computational creativity. Provides a working definition: "the philosophy, science and engineering of computational systems which, by taking on particular responsibilities, exhibit behaviours that unbiased observers would deem to be creative." Surveys seminal works and lays out research challenges.

## Key thesis
Computational creativity is now a recognized academic discipline with its own working definition, evaluation standards, and research agenda — not a side topic of AI. The community-consensus definition centers on the system *taking on creative responsibility*, not merely producing outputs that look creative.

## Key concepts
- **computational creativity (working definition)** — the canonical phrasing.
- **creative responsibility** — what distinguishes a creative system from a tool.
- **FACE model** (Pease et al.) — Framing, Aesthetics, Concept, Expression as creative acts the system performs.
- **IDEA evaluation** — Impact, Disruption, Effort, Audience.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator
The single best paper to cite when establishing "computational creativity" as a recognized field. ai-ideator is not a novel LLM trick; it sits in a 30-year academic tradition with its own evaluation standards. Reading this saves you from re-inventing definitions and metrics that already exist.
