## TL;DR

Comprehensive overview of ashwagandha (*Withania somnifera*), an Ayurvedic rasayana herb, covering its pharmacology, clinical evidence, and traditional uses. Relevant to gastritis: the review documents ashwagandha's gastroprotective effects in animal models, attributed to its anti-stress (adaptogenic) and anti-inflammatory properties via withanolide compounds. Connects the stress-gastritis link through an herbal medicine lens.

## Key concepts

- **Ashwagandha (*Withania somnifera*)** — Ayurvedic adaptogenic herb with documented anti-stress, anti-inflammatory, and gastroprotective effects.
- **Withanolides** — Steroidal lactones in ashwagandha responsible for many of its pharmacological activities.
- **Adaptogen** — Substance that normalizes physiological stress responses; relevant to stress-mediated gastric pathology.
