## TL;DR

Review examining how high dietary sugar intake shifts gut microbiota composition toward pro-inflammatory species and away from beneficial bacteria. Links the Western high-sugar diet to dysbiosis, increased intestinal permeability, and systemic inflammation. Relevant to gastritis as dysbiosis and mucosal inflammation are interconnected; dietary sugar may worsen gastric conditions through microbiome-mediated pathways.

## Key concepts

- **Sugar-induced dysbiosis** — High sugar intake promotes growth of pro-inflammatory gut bacteria at the expense of protective species.
- **Microbiome-inflammation axis** — Dietary sugar alters microbial balance, driving low-grade inflammation relevant to GI disease.
