## TL;DR

Review of clinical evidence for probiotics across GI disorders including H. pylori infection, antibiotic-associated diarrhea, IBS, and IBD. Concludes that specific strains show benefit as adjuncts to H. pylori triple therapy (improving eradication rates and reducing side effects) and in preventing antibiotic-associated diarrhea, while evidence for other GI indications is more mixed.

## Key concepts

- **Strain-specific effects** — Probiotic efficacy varies by species and strain; blanket recommendations are inappropriate.
- **H. pylori adjunct** — Certain probiotics (particularly *Saccharomyces boulardii* and *Lactobacillus* spp.) improve eradication rates and tolerability of triple therapy.
