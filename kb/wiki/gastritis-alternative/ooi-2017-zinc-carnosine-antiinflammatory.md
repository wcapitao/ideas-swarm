## TL;DR

In-vitro study demonstrating that zinc-L-carnosine suppresses NF-kB-mediated inflammatory signaling in colon fibroblasts and protects against DNA damage, promoting genomic stability. Provides mechanistic evidence for zinc-carnosine's anti-inflammatory properties beyond its gastroprotective effects, suggesting broader GI mucosal benefit.

## Key concepts

- **NF-kB inhibition** — Zinc-L-carnosine suppresses the NF-kB pathway, a master regulator of inflammatory gene expression.
- **Genomic stability** — Protective effect against DNA damage in GI mucosal cells, suggesting cancer-preventive potential.
