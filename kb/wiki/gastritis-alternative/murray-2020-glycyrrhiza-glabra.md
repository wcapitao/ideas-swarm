## TL;DR

Comprehensive textbook chapter reviewing the pharmacology and clinical evidence for *Glycyrrhiza glabra* (licorice) in gastrointestinal disease. Emphasizes deglycyrrhizinated licorice (DGL) as a mucosal protectant that stimulates mucus secretion and promotes healing of gastric and duodenal ulcers without the mineralocorticoid side effects of whole licorice.

## Key concepts

- **DGL (deglycyrrhizinated licorice)** — Licorice extract with glycyrrhizin removed to avoid aldosterone-like side effects; promotes mucosal defense.
- **Mucosal protection** — DGL stimulates mucus and prostaglandin production, enhancing the gastric mucosal barrier.
