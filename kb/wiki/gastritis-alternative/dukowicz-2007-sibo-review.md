## TL;DR

Comprehensive review of small intestinal bacterial overgrowth (SIBO), covering pathophysiology, diagnosis, and treatment. SIBO connects to gastritis through multiple pathways: hypochlorhydria (including PPI-induced) removes the gastric acid barrier against bacterial colonization; H. pylori-associated atrophic gastritis predisposes to SIBO; and SIBO itself can cause upper GI symptoms that mimic or exacerbate gastritis.

## Key concepts

- **SIBO** — Excessive bacterial colonization of the small intestine, causing malabsorption, bloating, and GI symptoms.
- **Gastric acid barrier** — Normal gastric acid secretion prevents bacterial overgrowth; its loss (via PPIs or atrophic gastritis) is a major SIBO risk factor.
- **Hydrogen breath test** — Primary non-invasive diagnostic tool for SIBO.
