## TL;DR

Review by the discoverer of zonulin, examining the role of increased intestinal permeability ("leaky gut") in the pathogenesis of chronic inflammatory and autoimmune diseases. Zonulin modulates tight junctions between enterocytes; its dysregulation allows luminal antigens to cross the epithelial barrier, triggering immune responses. Central to the functional medicine model of GI disease causation.

## Key concepts

- **Zonulin** — Protein that reversibly modulates intestinal tight junctions, increasing permeability when upregulated.
- **Leaky gut** — Increased intestinal permeability allowing translocation of antigens and bacteria, proposed as a precondition for autoimmune and inflammatory disease.
- **Tight junctions** — Intercellular structures controlling paracellular transport; their disruption is the mechanistic basis of leaky gut.
