## TL;DR

Accessible synthesis of the bidirectional gut-brain axis by a leading researcher in the field. Mayer explains how the enteric nervous system, vagus nerve, and gut microbiome communicate with the brain to influence mood, decision-making, and GI health. Directly relevant to gastritis: stress-mediated gut-brain signaling modulates gastric acid secretion, mucosal blood flow, and inflammatory responses, positioning psychosocial factors as genuine pathophysiological contributors rather than mere comorbidities.

## Key concepts

- **Gut-brain axis** — Bidirectional communication network between the GI tract and central nervous system via neural, hormonal, and immune pathways.
- **Enteric nervous system** — The "second brain" embedded in the GI wall, capable of autonomous function and influencing gastric physiology.
- **Microbiome-brain signaling** — Gut bacteria produce neurotransmitters and metabolites that influence brain function and behavior.
