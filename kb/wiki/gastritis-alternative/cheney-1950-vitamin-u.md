## TL;DR

Follow-up to Cheney's 1949 cabbage juice study, coining the term "vitamin U" for the anti-peptic ulcer dietary factor found in raw cabbage, celery, and other fresh vegetables. Reported continued clinical success with cabbage juice therapy and proposed that the active substance (later identified as S-methylmethionine) plays a role in maintaining gastric mucosal integrity.

## Key concepts

- **Vitamin U (S-methylmethionine)** — Amino acid derivative found in cruciferous vegetables, proposed as the active anti-ulcer component of cabbage juice.
- **Dietary anti-ulcer factor** — Concept that specific dietary compounds can promote gastric mucosal healing.
