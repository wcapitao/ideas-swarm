## TL;DR

Research-backed popular book by Stanford microbiologists arguing that the Western diet's fiber deficiency is starving beneficial gut microbes, leading to dysbiosis, inflammation, and chronic disease. Introduces the concept of microbiota-accessible carbohydrates (MACs) and advocates dietary strategies to restore microbial diversity. Relevant to gastritis through the fiber-microbiome-inflammation pathway.

## Key concepts

- **Microbiota-accessible carbohydrates (MACs)** — Dietary fibers that are metabolized by gut bacteria into beneficial short-chain fatty acids.
- **Fiber deficiency and dysbiosis** — Western diet's low fiber intake impoverishes microbial diversity, contributing to inflammatory conditions.
