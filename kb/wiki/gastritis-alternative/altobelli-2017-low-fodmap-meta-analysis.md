## TL;DR

Meta-analysis of RCTs confirming that a low-FODMAP diet significantly improves symptoms in irritable bowel syndrome, particularly bloating, abdominal pain, and overall symptom severity. While IBS-focused, the low-FODMAP approach is increasingly applied to functional dyspepsia and gastritis-adjacent symptoms, and the underlying fermentation-dysbiosis mechanism connects to broader alternative GI models.

## Key concepts

- **Low-FODMAP diet** — Dietary restriction of fermentable oligosaccharides, disaccharides, monosaccharides, and polyols to reduce GI symptom burden.
- **Fermentable carbohydrates** — Poorly absorbed short-chain carbohydrates that cause luminal distension and altered motility via bacterial fermentation.
