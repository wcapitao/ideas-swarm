## TL;DR

Ten-year cohort study on the diagnosis and management of achlorhydria (complete absence of gastric acid secretion). Documents the clinical presentation, associated conditions (pernicious anemia, atrophic gastritis), and treatment approaches of the era. Provides historical context for the later hypochlorhydria controversy and the shift toward acid suppression as the dominant GI paradigm.

## Key concepts

- **Achlorhydria** — Complete absence of hydrochloric acid in gastric secretions, associated with atrophic gastritis and pernicious anemia.
- **Historical diagnostic approach** — Pre-endoscopy era methods for assessing gastric acid output.
