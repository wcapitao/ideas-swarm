## TL;DR

Landmark Lancet meta-analysis comparing 110 homeopathy trials with 110 matched conventional medicine trials. Concluded that clinical effects of homeopathy are consistent with placebo, while conventional treatments showed significant effects beyond placebo. Larger, higher-quality homeopathy trials showed progressively smaller effects, suggesting bias and placebo response drive positive homeopathy results. This study is the standard reference for the scientific critique of homeopathy.

## Key concepts

- **Homeopathy and placebo** — Meta-analytic evidence that homeopathic treatments do not produce effects beyond placebo.
- **Trial quality gradient** — Larger and higher-quality homeopathy trials show smaller effect sizes, a hallmark of publication bias.
