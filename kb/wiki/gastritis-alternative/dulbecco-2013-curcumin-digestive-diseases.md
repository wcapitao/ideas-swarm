## TL;DR

Comprehensive review of curcumin's therapeutic potential across digestive diseases including gastritis, peptic ulcer, H. pylori infection, inflammatory bowel disease, and colorectal cancer. Summarizes evidence for curcumin's anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial mechanisms relevant to GI pathology, while noting bioavailability limitations.

## Key concepts

- **Curcumin** — Polyphenol from *Curcuma longa* (turmeric) with anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties relevant to GI disease.
- **Bioavailability challenge** — Poor oral bioavailability of curcumin limits systemic effects but may concentrate activity in the GI lumen.
