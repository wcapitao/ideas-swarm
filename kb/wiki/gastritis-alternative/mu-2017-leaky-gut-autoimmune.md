## TL;DR

Review examining the role of impaired intestinal barrier function ("leaky gut") as a trigger for autoimmune diseases. Discusses how microbiome dysbiosis, dietary factors, and environmental triggers can compromise the intestinal barrier, allowing microbial antigens to activate the immune system and potentially initiate autoimmune cascades. Relevant to the functional medicine model of gastritis as a systemic rather than purely local condition.

## Key concepts

- **Intestinal barrier dysfunction** — Breakdown of the epithelial barrier as a "danger signal" that precedes and enables autoimmune disease development.
- **Microbiome dysbiosis** — Altered gut microbial composition contributing to barrier breakdown and immune dysregulation.
