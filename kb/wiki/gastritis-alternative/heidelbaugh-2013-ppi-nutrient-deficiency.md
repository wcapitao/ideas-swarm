## TL;DR

Review of the evidence linking long-term proton pump inhibitor (PPI) use to nutrient deficiencies including magnesium, calcium, iron, vitamin B12, and vitamin C. Acid suppression impairs nutrient absorption that depends on gastric acid, raising concerns about chronic PPI use. This evidence is frequently cited by naturopathic and functional medicine practitioners to support the hypochlorhydria model and caution against prolonged acid suppression.

## Key concepts

- **PPI-induced nutrient deficiency** — Chronic acid suppression impairs absorption of acid-dependent nutrients (B12, iron, calcium, magnesium).
- **Long-term PPI risks** — Accumulating evidence of adverse effects from prolonged PPI use, including fracture risk and hypomagnesemia.
