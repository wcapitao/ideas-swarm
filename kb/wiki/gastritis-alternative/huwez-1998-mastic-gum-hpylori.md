## TL;DR

NEJM letter demonstrating that mastic gum (resin from *Pistacia lentiscus*) kills *H. pylori* in vitro at concentrations as low as 60 µg/mL. This early finding generated significant interest in mastic gum as a natural alternative to antibiotic-based H. pylori eradication, though subsequent in-vivo results have been more mixed.

## Key concepts

- **Mastic gum** — Resin from the *Pistacia lentiscus* tree, traditionally used in Mediterranean folk medicine for gastrointestinal complaints.
- **H. pylori bactericidal activity** — In-vitro killing of H. pylori at low concentrations, suggesting potential therapeutic use.
