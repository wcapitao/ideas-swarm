## TL;DR

Martin Blaser's "Missing Microbes" (2014) presents the provocative counter-thesis to universal H. pylori eradication: that H. pylori is an amphibiont (sometimes harmful, sometimes beneficial) that has co-evolved with humans for at least 60,000 years, and its disappearance may be driving increases in esophageal adenocarcinoma, childhood asthma, and obesity. This work represents the most prominent challenge to the Kyoto consensus and forces a more nuanced view of the gastritis-H. pylori relationship — not simply "infection to be eradicated" but a complex host-microbe equilibrium.
