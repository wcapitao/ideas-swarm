## TL;DR

The OLGA (2007) and OLGIM (2008) staging systems translate the histological findings of the Sydney System into a cancer risk stratification framework. By mapping the extent and severity of atrophy (OLGA) or intestinal metaplasia (OLGIM) across antral and corpus biopsies into stages 0-IV, they provide clinicians with an actionable risk score: stages III-IV carry significantly elevated gastric cancer risk and warrant surveillance. These systems operationalize the Correa cascade into a clinical decision tool.
