## TL;DR

Sleisenger and Fordtran's — known as the "GI Bible" — is the most comprehensive single-volume reference on gastrointestinal and liver disease. Its gastritis chapters provide deeper specialist-level coverage than Harrison's, including detailed pathophysiology, histopathological interpretation, and management algorithms. For the KB, it serves as the authoritative conventional gastroenterology reference for any claim about gastritis mechanisms, diagnosis, or treatment.
