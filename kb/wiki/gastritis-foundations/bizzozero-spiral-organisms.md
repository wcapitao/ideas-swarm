## TL;DR

Giulio Bizzozero's 1893 observation of spiral organisms in the gastric mucosa of dogs represents the first documented sighting of what would later be identified as Helicobacter. This discovery was ignored for 90 years — buried under the "no acid, no ulcer" dogma and the assumption that the stomach was too acidic for bacterial colonization. Bizzozero's forgotten finding is a canonical example of how paradigmatic assumptions can suppress empirical observations, directly relevant to understanding why Marshall and Warren's 1982 rediscovery was initially met with ridicule.
