## TL;DR

Knud Faber's 1935 work systematically established the link between chronic atrophic gastritis, achlorhydria, and pernicious anemia through longitudinal observation of patient cohorts. By tracing the progression from chronic inflammation to glandular atrophy to systemic disease, Faber laid the groundwork for understanding gastritis as a progressive, consequential condition rather than a benign inflammatory nuisance — a framing that would later prove essential to Correa's carcinogenesis cascade model.
