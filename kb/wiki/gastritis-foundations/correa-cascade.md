## TL;DR

Pelayo Correa's 1988 paper proposed the multistep cascade model of gastric carcinogenesis: normal mucosa -> chronic superficial gastritis -> chronic atrophic gastritis -> intestinal metaplasia -> dysplasia -> adenocarcinoma. This model unified decades of epidemiological and pathological observations into a single progressive framework that explained why chronic gastritis matters — it is not just inflammation but the first step in a potentially lethal sequence. The Correa cascade remains the conceptual backbone of gastric cancer prevention and surveillance strategies worldwide.
