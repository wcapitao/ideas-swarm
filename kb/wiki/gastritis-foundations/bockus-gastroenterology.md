## TL;DR

Bockus' Gastroenterology (1943) was one of the first comprehensive textbooks dedicated to gastrointestinal disease as a subspecialty, marking the emergence of gastroenterology as a distinct medical discipline. Its gastritis sections capture the state of knowledge in the pre-biopsy, early endoscopy era — relying heavily on Schindler's visual classification and clinical correlation. It serves as a historical benchmark for how gastritis was understood before the histological and microbiological revolutions.
