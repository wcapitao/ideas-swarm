## TL;DR

The Huangdi Neijing (~200 BCE) is the foundational text of Traditional Chinese Medicine and establishes the theoretical framework for understanding gastric disease through the Wei (Stomach) and Pi (Spleen) organ systems. It describes stomach heat patterns, qi stagnation, and cold invasion as mechanisms of gastric dysfunction — a paradigm that remains the basis of TCM gastritis treatment today. The text's systematic organ-correspondence model offers a fundamentally different ontology of gastric disease from both Western and Ayurvedic traditions.
