## TL;DR

Karl Schwarz's 1910 dictum "Keine Säure — kein Ulcus" ("No acid, no ulcer") became the defining dogma of 20th-century gastroenterology, directing decades of research and treatment toward acid suppression. While correct that acid is necessary for ulceration, the dictum suppressed investigation into the bacterial etiology of gastritis and ulcer disease for over 70 years. It represents one of medicine's most consequential examples of a partially correct insight hardening into a paradigmatic barrier that blocked the H. pylori revolution.
