## TL;DR

Rudolf Schindler's 1947 monograph "Gastritis" represents the first systematic visual classification of gastric mucosal inflammation, made possible by his pioneering development of the semi-flexible gastroscope. Schindler — widely regarded as the father of gastroscopy — established that gastritis could be directly observed and classified rather than inferred from symptoms alone. His visual grading system was the direct precursor to the Sydney System and all subsequent endoscopic-histological classification schemes.
