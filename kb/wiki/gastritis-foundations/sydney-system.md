## TL;DR

The Updated Sydney System (Dixon et al., 1996; originally Misiewicz, 1990) is the international standard for histological classification and grading of gastritis. It introduced a standardized five-biopsy protocol, visual analogue scales for grading inflammation/activity/atrophy/intestinal metaplasia/H. pylori density, and a combined topographic-etiological-morphological framework. Virtually all subsequent gastritis classification systems (OLGA, OLGIM, Kyoto) build on the Sydney System's foundation.
