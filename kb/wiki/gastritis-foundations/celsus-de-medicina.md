## TL;DR

Celsus' De Medicina (~25-35 CE) is the oldest surviving complete medical text in Latin and provides one of the earliest clinical descriptions of stomach inflammation and its dietary management. Celsus systematized Greek medical knowledge for Roman practitioners, describing gastric symptoms with a clinical precision that anticipated modern descriptive gastroenterology. His emphasis on observation over theory and his practical dietary recommendations make him a bridge between Hippocratic humoral medicine and later empirical approaches.
