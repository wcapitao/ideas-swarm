## TL;DR

William Osler's "Principles and Practice of Medicine" (1892) was the most influential English-language medical textbook of its era. Its section on "Catarrhal Gastritis" captures the late 19th-century understanding of gastric inflammation — diagnosed clinically without endoscopy, classified by acuity, and treated with dietary restriction and bismuth. Osler's framing represents the last major synthesis before endoscopy and histology transformed gastritis from a clinical syndrome into a tissue diagnosis.
