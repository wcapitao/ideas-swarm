## TL;DR

William Beaumont's 1833 monograph is the founding work of gastric physiology, based on direct observation of digestion through the gastric fistula of Alexis St. Martin. Beaumont documented the composition and action of gastric juice, the appearance of the stomach lining under various conditions, and the effect of emotions on gastric function. This work transformed gastric disease from a humoral abstraction into an observable, measurable physiological process and inaugurated the modern era of gastroenterology.
