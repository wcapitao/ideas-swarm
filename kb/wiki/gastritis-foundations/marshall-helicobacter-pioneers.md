## TL;DR

Barry Marshall's edited volume collects firsthand accounts from the scientists involved in discovering Helicobacter pylori and establishing its role in gastritis and ulcer disease. The book documents the paradigm resistance, institutional skepticism, and personal perseverance that characterized one of medicine's most dramatic revolutions. As a primary source on how scientific paradigm shifts actually happen in gastroenterology, it provides unique insight into why the bacterial etiology of gastritis was resisted for decades despite accumulating evidence.
