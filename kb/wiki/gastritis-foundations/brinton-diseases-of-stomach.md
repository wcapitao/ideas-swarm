## TL;DR

Brinton's 1859 treatise is one of the earliest systematic monographs devoted entirely to stomach diseases, consolidating mid-19th-century knowledge of gastric pathology. It established the clinical-pathological correlation approach to gastritis classification — distinguishing acute from chronic forms and attempting to link symptoms to anatomical findings — that would remain the dominant framework until endoscopy and histology transformed the field a century later.
