## TL;DR

Avicenna's Canon of Medicine (1025 CE) is the most influential medical encyclopedia of the Islamic Golden Age and was used as a medical textbook in Europe through the 17th century. Its section on stomach diseases provides a detailed humoral classification of gastric disorders, including inflammation, with systematic differential diagnosis and compound herbal remedies. The Canon represents the most sophisticated pre-modern synthesis of Greco-Roman, Persian, and Indian medical knowledge applied to gastric disease.
