## TL;DR

Dioscorides' De Materia Medica (~50-70 CE) is the most influential pharmacological text of antiquity, cataloguing ~600 plants and their medicinal uses. For gastric ailments, it documents mastic, licorice, wormwood, and aloe — remedies that have persisted across two millennia and are now subjects of modern pharmacological research. The text represents the earliest systematic materia medica for stomach disorders and serves as the bridge between Hippocratic dietary therapy and later herbal gastric treatments.
