## TL;DR

Strickland and Mackay's 1973 paper introduced the Type A / Type B classification of chronic atrophic gastritis — Type A being autoimmune (corpus-predominant, associated with parietal cell antibodies and pernicious anemia) and Type B being environmental/bacterial (antral-predominant). This binary framework was the dominant gastritis classification for two decades until the Sydney System, and it established the critical insight that gastritis is not one disease but at least two distinct entities with different etiologies, topographies, and clinical consequences.
