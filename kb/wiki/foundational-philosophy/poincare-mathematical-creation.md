## TL;DR
Mathematical discovery proceeds in stages: conscious **preparation**, an unconscious **incubation** in which the mind combines ideas in vast numbers, an aesthetic **sieve** that lets only the elegant combinations into consciousness, and finally **verification**. The first explicit articulation of creativity as massive subconscious combinatorial search filtered by an aesthetic / utility criterion.

## Key thesis
The conscious mind cannot enumerate the vast space of possible mathematical combinations; the unconscious does it. But the unconscious is not random — it is filtered by a sense of mathematical beauty/utility, which acts as the selection criterion. Only combinations that pass this filter surface to consciousness as candidate solutions.

## Key concepts
- **unconscious combinatorial work** — large-scale shuffling of ideas below awareness.
- **aesthetic sieve / sense of beauty** — the filter that decides which combinations surface.
- **fertile combinations from distant domains** — Poincaré's specific insight: the most productive combinations join ideas from far-apart areas.
- **atoms hooked together** — Poincaré's image of how ideas form stable wholes.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator
Poincaré is the *architectural prototype* of an LLM ideation agent: massive candidate generation + a beauty/utility-based scoring function that surfaces a small, high-quality subset. He also names the most important empirical regularity: the most fertile combinations come from **domains that are far apart** — direct evidence for distance-controlled retrieval (Mednick's remote associates) being a value-creating mechanism.

## Memorable passages
> "Ideas rose in crowds; I felt them collide until pairs interlocked, so to speak, making a stable combination… Among chosen combinations the most fertile will often be those formed of elements drawn from domains which are far apart."
