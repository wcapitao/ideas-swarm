## TL;DR
James reframes association: it is not ideas but *brain-processes* that associate. He proposes one fundamental law of contiguity (with similarity as a derivative case) and explains creative thought as guided by **interest** selecting among partial activations — directly prefiguring variation-and-selection accounts of creativity.

## Key thesis
The lawful unit is brain-process redintegration: when two elementary brain-processes have been active together, one tends to propagate excitement into the other. But association alone overproduces; what guides which associates surface to consciousness is *interest* — the goal-directed selectivity of attention. Creative thought is association *under selection*.

## Key concepts
- **redintegration** — re-instatement of a whole pattern from a part.
- **law of contiguity** — the one fundamental law; similarity is derivative.
- **partial recall** — only some associates ever surface; selection is necessary.
- **interest** — the principle that prunes the combinatorial explosion.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator
James updates Hume for the era of brain science and introduces the idea that **selection (driven by interest) prunes the combinatorial explosion of associations**. This is exactly the architecture of an LLM ideation agent: cheap massive generation + goal-directed scoring. James names what Boden later formalizes (P-creativity vs H-creativity) and what Campbell formalizes as BVSR.

## Memorable passages
> "There is no other elementary causal law of association than the law of neural habit: When two elementary brain-processes have been active together or in immediate succession, one of them, on reoccurring, tends to propagate its excitement into the other." — Vol. I, Ch. XIV
