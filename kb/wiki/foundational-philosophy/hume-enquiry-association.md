## TL;DR
A more accessible restatement of the *Treatise*'s three associative principles — resemblance, contiguity in time or place, and cause/effect — positioned as the universal laws by which thought moves. The Enquiry is the canonical short-form citation in cognitive-science literature.

## Key thesis
The same three-fold taxonomy as the Treatise, but condensed and in Hume's mature, public-facing prose. The Enquiry frames association as a fundamental fact about how minds work, suitable for grounding a science of human understanding.

## Key concepts
- **principles of connexion** — Hume's mature term for the laws of association.
- **resemblance / contiguity / causation** — the three exclusive principles.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator
Useful as the "public" source for the associationist vocabulary; it is more often cited than the Treatise in modern cognitive-science papers and is the cleanest one-sentence statement of the three-operator retrieval model.

## Memorable passages
> "To me, there appear to be only three principles of connexion among ideas, namely, Resemblance, Contiguity in time or place, and Cause or Effect."
