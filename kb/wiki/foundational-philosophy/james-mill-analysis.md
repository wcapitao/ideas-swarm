## TL;DR
James Mill pushes associationism to its strictest form: all mental life is built from sensations and ideas welded by association. J.S. Mill's notes amend this with the doctrine of **mental chemistry** — that complex ideas can have *emergent* properties not reducible to their components.

## Key thesis
James Mill: ideas combine like atoms — laws of association deterministically produce all higher mental phenomena. J.S. Mill (in the 1869 edition's notes): some combinations are not arithmetic but chemical — the resulting compound has properties not predictable from the parts.

## Key concepts
- **mental chemistry** (J.S. Mill) — combinations whose properties exceed the sum of components.
- **indissoluble association** — links so strong they feel necessary.
- **compound ideas** — emergent unities built from simpler ideas.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator
J.S. Mill's mental-chemistry metaphor is the precise philosophical move that LLM combinatorial systems need: combinations of concepts can yield wholes whose meaning is *not* a linear sum of parts. This is the bridge from atomistic association (mere shuffling) to genuinely emergent ideation. It's also the empirical promise of conceptual blending: the blend has structure neither input had.

## Memorable passages
> "The laws of the phenomena of mind are sometimes analogous to mechanical, but sometimes also to chemical laws… the simple ideas generate, rather than compose, the complex ones." — J.S. Mill, *A System of Logic* VI.iv.3
