## TL;DR
Hartley fuses Locke's association of ideas with a physiological "doctrine of vibrations": simple ideas, repeatedly co-occurring, fuse into complex ideas via lawful neural processes. The first systematic mechanistic associationism — direct ancestor of Hebbian learning and connectionism.

## Key thesis
Mind and brain run in parallel: every idea has a corresponding neural vibration. When two vibrations repeatedly co-occur, they associate; the corresponding ideas become inseparable. Higher-order ideas are lawful products of this co-activation.

## Key concepts
- **doctrine of vibrations** — physical correlate of mental association.
- **vibratiuncles** — small persistent vibrations that constitute memory traces.
- **simple → complex ideas** — compound ideas are products of repeated co-association.
- **psychophysical parallelism** — every mental event has a neural correlate.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator
Hartley turns association from a philosophical observation into a *mechanism*. The historical analogue of LLM embeddings: repeated co-occurrence in a corpus produces fused vector representations. When we say "concepts are nearby in embedding space," we are cashing out Hartley's vibratiuncles in linear algebra.

## Memorable passages
> "Any sensations A, B, C, etc., by being associated with one another a sufficient number of times, get such a power over the corresponding ideas a, b, c, etc., that any one of the sensations A, when impressed alone, shall be able to excite in the mind b, c, etc., the ideas of the rest."
