## TL;DR
Hume reduces the principles of mental association to **three**: resemblance, contiguity in time/place, and cause-and-effect. These act as a "gentle force" that uniformly shapes how the imagination moves from one idea to another. This is the single most influential taxonomy of association in the history of cognitive science.

## Key thesis
Without some lawful tendency, ideas would float free; chance alone could not produce the orderly transitions of thought. Hume names three such laws — and only three — by which the imagination spontaneously moves between ideas. They are not necessary connections but tendencies; hence the "gentle force" image.

## Key concepts
- **resemblance** — moving from an idea to one that is similar.
- **contiguity** — moving from an idea to one that was near it in space/time.
- **cause and effect** — moving from cause-idea to effect-idea or vice versa.
- **gentle force** — Hume's metaphor: association is reliable but not deterministic.
- **impressions vs. ideas** — sensations are primary; ideas are their fainter copies.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator
Hume's three principles map directly onto the retrieval operators of any LLM ideation pipeline:
- *resemblance* → embedding-similarity retrieval
- *contiguity* → co-occurrence retrieval (concepts that appeared in similar contexts)
- *cause and effect* → causal-graph retrieval

Any retrieval-and-combination system implicitly chooses among these three operators; making the choice **explicit** grounds the system in a 300-year tradition and lets you reason about which operator yields which kind of blend.

## Memorable passages
> "Were ideas entirely loose and unconnected, chance alone would join them… we are therefore to regard [association] as a gentle force, which commonly prevails." — *Treatise* I.i.iv
