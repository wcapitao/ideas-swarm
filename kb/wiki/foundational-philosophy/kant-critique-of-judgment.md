## TL;DR
Kant distinguishes a merely **reproductive imagination** (which recombines what was perceived) from a **productive imagination** of genius, which generates "aesthetic ideas" that bring the cognitive faculties into free play and exceed any concept that could be given. This is the classical philosophical statement of why mere recombination is not yet creativity.

## Key thesis
Genius is the natural endowment by which "nature gives the rule to art." Its products are exemplary — they could not have been derived by following rules, yet they generate new rules others can recognize. Reproductive imagination merely shuffles given material; productive imagination *originates*.

## Key concepts
- **productive vs. reproductive imagination** — origination vs. recombination.
- **aesthetic ideas** — representations of the imagination that can never be fully captured by concepts.
- **free play of the faculties** — the harmony between imagination and understanding that constitutes aesthetic experience.
- **exemplary originality** — the genius's work is original *and* sets a pattern for others.

## Why it matters for ai-ideator
Kant names the deepest design tension in LLM creativity: most LLM "creativity" is reproductive recombination — it shuffles what was in training data. The Kantian question is what would have to be different for the system to be productive. This is the central audit our evaluator must perform: does the candidate idea exceed mere recombination of recognizable parts?

## Memorable passages
> "Genius is the talent (natural endowment) which gives the rule to art… Genius is the innate mental disposition (ingenium) through which nature gives the rule to art." — §46
