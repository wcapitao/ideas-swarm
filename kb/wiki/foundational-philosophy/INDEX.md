# Foundational Philosophy — Index

> The intellectual lineage from Locke (1690) to Boden (1990): the philosophical and proto-cognitive-science roots of the idea that creativity = combination of existing ideas under selection.

Last updated: 2026-04-30
Articles: 13

## T1 — Foundational

| Article | Authors | Year | Summary |
|---|---|---|---|
| [locke-association-of-ideas](locke-association-of-ideas.md) | Locke | 1690 | Coins "association of ideas." Distinguishes natural-correspondence from chance/custom connection. |
| [hume-treatise-association-of-ideas](hume-treatise-association-of-ideas.md) | Hume | 1739 | The three principles: resemblance, contiguity, cause-and-effect — a "gentle force" governing imagination. |
| [kant-critique-of-judgment](kant-critique-of-judgment.md) | Kant | 1790 | Productive vs reproductive imagination; aesthetic ideas; genius as the "rule-giver to art." |
| [coleridge-biographia-literaria](coleridge-biographia-literaria.md) | Coleridge | 1817 | Fancy (mechanical recombination) vs Imagination (esemplastic, dissolves and recreates). |
| [james-principles-association](james-principles-association.md) | W. James | 1890 | Brain-process redintegration; one law of contiguity; interest selects among partial activations. |
| [poincare-mathematical-creation](poincare-mathematical-creation.md) | Poincaré | 1908 | Massive subconscious combinatorial work + aesthetic sieve = mathematical discovery. Architectural prototype of an ideation agent. |
| [wallas-art-of-thought](wallas-art-of-thought.md) | Wallas | 1926 | The four-stage model: preparation, incubation, illumination, verification. Workflow template for any creativity system. |
| [campbell-bvsr](campbell-bvsr.md) | Campbell | 1960 | Blind Variation and Selective Retention — Darwinian model of creative thought; the philosophical underpinning of generate-and-test. |
| [mednick-associative-basis](mednick-associative-basis.md) | Mednick | 1962 | Flat vs steep associative hierarchies; Remote Associates Test; remote combinations are more creative. |
| [koestler-act-of-creation](koestler-act-of-creation.md) | Koestler | 1964 | Bisociation — the simultaneous activity of two normally-separate matrices on one situation. **The single most important reference.** |
