## TL;DR
Coleridge sharply separates **Fancy** — a mechanical re-arrangement of fixed and dead memories — from **Imagination**, a vital, synthetic, "esemplastic" (shaping-into-one) power that fuses opposites into a new organic unity. The literary-critical version of Kant's reproductive/productive distinction, and the cleanest articulation of why mere combination is not enough.

## Key thesis
Imagination is not a higher form of memory. Fancy can only juxtapose what already exists in fixed form; Imagination dissolves, diffuses, and reconstitutes — it has the power to generate an organic whole that is not simply the arithmetic of its parts.

## Key concepts
- **Fancy** — mechanical recombination of pre-formed materials; what association alone can produce.
- **Primary Imagination** — the living power of perception itself; the "prime agent of all human perception."
- **Secondary Imagination** — the conscious, voluntary version that produces art; "dissolves, diffuses, dissipates, in order to recreate."
- **Esemplastic power** — Coleridge's coinage from Greek roots: "shaping into one."

## Why it matters for ai-ideator
An LLM that only juxtaposes concepts is operating at the level of Fancy. Coleridge names what would make the leap to Imagination: not more juxtaposition but **dissolution and recreation** — the input concepts must be decomposed, their structure abstracted, and a new whole generated that re-organizes the material. This is the architectural argument for adding a "generic space" / abstraction step (Fauconnier-Turner) between retrieval and blending.

## Memorable passages
> "FANCY… has no other counters to play with, but fixities and definites… it must receive all its materials ready made from the law of association." — Ch. XIII

> "The Imagination… dissolves, diffuses, dissipates, in order to recreate." — Ch. XIII
