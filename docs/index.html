The Pipeline

How It Works

From a plain-language problem description to evaluated, traceable diagnostic hypotheses.

1

Describe the Problem

A patient, caregiver, or doctor describes a medical problem in plain language. Examples: "My child has a recurring rash after eating certain foods" or "Post-surgical patient showing unexpected fever patterns."

2

Knowledge Retrieval

The system searches a curated medical knowledge base — research papers, clinical guidelines, pharmacology references, diagnostic patterns — and retrieves relevant medical concepts. Unlike a simple search engine, it deliberately pulls concepts from distant domains too (e.g., connecting an immunology concept with a nutrition pattern).

3

Combinatorial Blending

This is the core innovation. The AI engine combines retrieved concepts using conceptual blending — a cognitive science technique that creates new ideas by finding emergent structure between unrelated concepts. It generates multiple diagnostic hypotheses and potential solutions, each fully traceable to its source concepts.

4

Evaluate & Rank

A council of specialized AI evaluators scores each hypothesis on: clinical plausibility, patient safety, novelty (is this a known connection or genuinely new?), and actionability (can the patient/doctor act on this?). Only ideas that pass all evaluators are presented.

Warning — MedIdeator is a research tool that generates hypotheses for exploration. It does not replace professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult a qualified healthcare provider.
